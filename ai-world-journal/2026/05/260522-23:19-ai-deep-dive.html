6. Cybersecurity implications

  • AI agents become attack surface: prompt injection is no longer just model behavior; it becomes permission abuse, data exfiltration and workflow manipulation.
  • Non-human identity governance: each agent needs owner, scope, credentials, logs, kill switch and periodic access review.
  • Supply-chain opacity: model APIs, plugins, vector DBs, telemetry and fine-tuning data must enter supplier risk files.
  • AI cyber arms race: offensive automation, phishing quality and vulnerability triage speed keep rising.
Actionable rule: do not procure “agentic” systems without an identity-and-access model, audit logs, human escalation thresholds and incident response playbook.

7. Post-quantum and systemic risks

  • Post-quantum risk: AI accelerates data aggregation; “harvest now, decrypt later” becomes more damaging when datasets are enriched by AI.
  • Lock-in/dependency: model APIs, proprietary agent frameworks and cloud-specific orchestration can create hidden switching costs.
  • Trust risk: AI-generated decisions without explainability weaken institutional legitimacy, especially in public services.
  • Energy/compute constraints: datacenter expansion ties AI policy to grid capacity, cooling, permitting and strategic energy planning.

Governance

Cyber

  • Treat agents as privileged non-human users.
  • Mandate MFA-equivalent controls, scoped credentials and revocation.
  • Require incident notification for model/data/security events.

Gelre turns mandatory ICU training into a workflow-adoption signal

Market signal: Zorgvisie reports that Gelre nurses use a more engaging escape-room format for recurring mandatory ICU training.

Truviq angle: Training and adoption are often the weak link in digital transformation; orchestration angle is around assigning, evidencing and escalating mandatory training, protocol changes and adoption tasks.

Evidence: Medium-low — concrete account example, but it may be an HR/learning intervention rather than an orchestration buying trigger.

NL2026-05-25Care workforce / onboarding / retention

New-care-worker churn highlights onboarding and expectation-management workflow risk

Market signal: Zorgvisie reports that many care workers leave within six months when the actual work differs from recruitment expectations.

Truviq angle: Hypothesis: larger care organisations can improve onboarding by orchestrating realistic job previews, credentialing, induction tasks, manager check-ins, early-warning signals and HR/ECD/planning hand-offs.

Evidence: Medium-low — sector-media signal; workflow technology demand must be validated per provider.

ZORG-ID Smartcard cannot yet broadly replace UZI provider passes

Truviq angle: Access modernisation remains transitional: care organisations may need governed processes for credential choices, role validation, exceptions, communication and migration planning.

Evidence: High for official constraint; medium-low as a commercial trigger without named implementation programme.

Demolish check — what could weaken this analysis?

Procurement risk: several sources are policy or ecosystem signals, not direct buying intent. Validate named budget owners before outreach.

Vendor-landscape risk: existing EHR/ECD, integration and managed-service suppliers may already own parts of the workflow layer.

Evidence risk: some Dutch sector items come from media summaries; official policy, tender and account sources should be checked before strong prioritisation.

Positioning risk: Camunda/process orchestration should be framed as an EHR-adjacent control layer, not as a replacement for incumbent clinical systems.

Recommended next moves

1. Validate the AZWA/transformation-funding route: official documents, model-plan owners and insurer programme teams.

2. Split account types: direct buyers, ecosystem influencers, implementation partners and regulatory/tender monitors.

3. For high-priority records, collect named transformation programmes, procurement signals, technology stack evidence and likely buyer personas.

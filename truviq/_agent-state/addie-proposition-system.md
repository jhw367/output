# Addie — Autonomous Proposition Definition & Optimization System

Status: active process layer for the Truviq NL/EU health-market research agents.

Purpose: turn Truviq’s EU healthcare automation proposition into a living, evidence-based, self-optimizing asset: localized trust + remote scale + sovereign AI + SSR-validated synthetic stakeholder insight + AI Act/GDPR/EHDS readiness.

## Initial focus

1. Netherlands pilot cycle first.
2. Scale later to Germany, France, Nordics, Belgium and other EU health markets only after the NL workflow/proposition loop is concrete.
3. Keep the existing Truviq rule: no broad scans until each vertical has at least two workflows with owner, pain, budget/cost hypothesis, alternatives and delivery-channel route.

## Proposition pillars to test

- Efficiency gains in administrative workflows: prior authorization, claims processing, care management, BPM/orchestration, exception routing and reporting.
- Data sovereignty: local/EU VPS, EU-hosted/open-source models where possible, privacy-preserving deployment.
- Managed service model: remote scale with local governance, support and partner front-end.
- Local partner model: language, procurement trust, implementation governance and healthcare credibility.
- Multilingual support and cultural localization.
- High-risk AI Act readiness: risk management, data quality, logging, human oversight, transparency, conformity assessment and audit evidence.

## Agent roles

- Researcher Agent: market, regulatory, competitor and workflow intelligence.
- Compliance Guardian Agent: AI Act, GDPR, EHDS and national healthcare-rule monitoring.
- SSR Simulator Agent: synthetic stakeholder interviews and semantic-similarity rating.
- Proposition Architect Agent: value proposition, features, pricing, delivery model and messaging.
- Evaluator Agent: LLM-as-judge plus metrics and previous-version benchmark.
- Optimizer / Self-Improver Agent: analyzes gaps, improves prompts, variants, persona library and evaluation rules.
- Partner & Localization Agent: tests local partner/channel fit and language/cultural adaptation.
- Orchestrator / Memory Manager: coordinates runs, maintains this state, version history and action next steps.

## Continuous loop

Each Addie cycle follows:

1. Observe: update market/regulatory/competitor intelligence for one narrow hypothesis or proposition element.
2. Simulate: run a lightweight SSR thought experiment for sampled personas; local language + English where useful.
3. Synthesize: update one proposition component, objection handler, pilot design or feature priority.
4. Evaluate: score market alignment, SSR appeal, compliance completeness, differentiation/feasibility, localization quality and risk coverage.
5. Improve: identify weak area, update next test, variant, persona or evidence requirement.
6. Deploy/test: produce one recommended next action, partner question, pilot test or sales asset delta.

## SSR method

Maintain/expand a persona library over time. Desired eventual depth: 50–200+ personas per country. For each run, keep it lightweight unless explicitly asked for a deep cycle:

- Sample/generate only enough personas to test the selected hypothesis or proposition variant.
- Roles: hospital admin, insurer IT/operations manager, GP/practice manager, patient advocate, regional authority, compliance officer, CIO/CMIO, care-manager, procurement/implementation partner.
- Capture demographics/context: region, tech maturity, privacy attitude, procurement conservatism, pain intensity, language/culture.
- Prompt style: detailed profile + Truviq concept variant + unfiltered inner monologue, concerns, excitement, questions and decision factors.
- Convert thoughts into Likert-like scores for purchase intent, trust/privacy, expected time savings, compliance confidence, integration ease, likelihood to pilot and barriers.
- Treat SSR as hypothesis generation, not proof. Real-world sources and stakeholder feedback outrank synthetic results.

## Evaluation metrics

Score 1–10 or percentage where useful:

- Market-priority alignment, weighted by recency and importance.
- SSR-derived appeal and realistic variance.
- Compliance completeness: AI Act/GDPR/EHDS checklist coverage.
- Differentiation and feasibility: Truviq automation depth + managed scale + local trust.
- Localization quality and channel risk.
- Evidence strength and actionability.

Always include a demolish check: why the proposition/hypothesis may be wrong, what evidence is weak, and what would disconfirm it.

Sprint-plan changes

Sprint 2 — account evidence

Top-15 selection starts with these 8 plotted leads. Each lead must have programme, procurement signal, stack evidence, persona and disqualifier before Sales-People calls.

Sprint 4 — Sales-People pilot

Pilot should call 10 records: the ranked leads above plus 2 alternates. Required note fields: named contact, role, programme, confirmed pain, stack, buying route, HLTH availability and next step.

Sprint 7/8 — event to discovery

A-leads must leave HLTH with a booked workflow-fit session. B+/B leads get partner-intro or validation call within 5 business days.

HLTH operating model

  1. Pre-event: Sales-People validates contacts and books meetings; Truviq leadership handles strategic ecosystem accounts.
  2. At event: classify every conversation as buyer, influencer, partner, tender monitor or noise.
  3. After event: select 3 proposal candidates only if sponsor, process owner, programme and procurement route are named.

Demolish check

  • Several high-scoring leads are ecosystem routes, not direct buyers; success depends on introductions and partner-led routes.
  • AZWA model plans may standardise spend in ways that reduce bespoke Camunda opportunity.
  • Health executives will reject generic event outreach; every call must reference the named programme and workflow.
  • Sales-People may secure access but should not explain architecture or policy detail.

Named-stakeholder sourcing guardrail

Named people above are public organisation-level stakeholders from official organisation pages where available. They are not automatically the operational buyer. Treat them as routing, governance or validation names; the first Sales-People/Truviq call must still identify the actual programme owner, budget owner and implementation owner.

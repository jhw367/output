Sales-People evidence

Source: https://www.sales-people.nl/

Sales-People positions itself around B2B telephone lead generation, appointment setting, lead follow-up, relationship management, aftersales, quote follow-up and marketing follow-up. Its core claim is personal contact rather than automated funnels: it acts as a remote extension of the client's sales team, plans appointments directly into calendars, and focuses on interested prospects.

How Sales-People could help Truviq

Best-fit role

Commercial execution partner for high-trust Dutch health outreach, especially where Truviq has a strong evidence-based account thesis but lacks warm entry into programme owners.

Concrete June/HLTH Europe use cases

  • Pre-event appointment setting for HLTH Europe Amsterdam, 15-18 June 2026, RAI Amsterdam.
  • Follow-up calling to Dutch Pavilion / HLTH attendee prospects after Truviq sends a highly specific message around process orchestration for AI, digital triage, capacity planning, EHDS, consent and cyber resilience.
  • Reactivation of existing Camunda/Truviq contacts who have health-sector relevance.
  • Qualifying whether high-priority records have named programmes, budget route, incumbent platforms and right buyer persona.
  • Booking short discovery calls before/after HLTH instead of trying to sell complex workflow transformation on the first call.

Suggested campaign design

  1. Build a 50-account health priority list: 20 direct buyers, 10 insurer/programme teams, 10 ecosystem influencers, 10 implementation partners/vendors.
  2. Write 4 call tracks by account type:
  • direct buyer: operational bottleneck + transformation programme;
  • ecosystem influencer: validate programme ownership + ask for right implementation contact;
  • implementation partner: explore co-selling/referral;
  • tender monitor: ask about upcoming procurement windows and public programme artefacts.
  1. Use Sales-People for human qualification, not generic cold selling.
  2. Feed every call note back into the Truviq dataset: programme, signal, stack, persona, next action, disqualifier.
  3. Use HLTH as the deadline: “can we schedule 20 minutes around HLTH Amsterdam?” is easier than “can we sell you orchestration?”.
  4. Use the NL Health~Holland Pavilion as a routing filter, not a buyer list: ask whether the organisation is exhibiting/attending through the pavilion, which provider/payer/programme owner they want to meet, and what post-event workflow follow-up is intended.

  • Add timing/urgency: event date, consultation deadline, publication due date, procurement calendar, programme milestone.
  • Add source strength levels separately for policy, procurement, technology stack and named-person evidence.
  • Add CRM-ready next action: owner, due date, outreach angle, suggested ask, desired evidence.
  • Add named-person evidence only when sourced from official pages/LinkedIn/manual validation; do not infer names from roles.
  • Add partner map around HLTH: TFHC, Health~Holland, RVO, regional development agencies, pavilion exhibitors, Camunda partners, health-IT suppliers.
  • Add message-market fit testing: compare call response for four hooks — AZWA model plans, AI governance, consent/identity, cyber/process continuity.
  • Add compliance guardrails: GDPR-safe call scripts, opt-out logging, no patient data, no implication of insurer endorsement.

15–18 June HLTH call-to-meeting operating script

  • Opening frame: “We are preparing 20-minute HLTH/June workflow-fit conversations for Truviq around [sourced programme hook]. Who owns the operational workflow or programme route on your side?”
  • Do ask: owner, programme name, current platform/supplier, timing/procurement clue, HLTH attendance, NL Health~Holland Pavilion/exhibitor route, desired provider/payer meeting, whether a short validation meeting is useful.
  • Do not ask: patient data, confidential project data, broad digital-transformation opinions, or vendor-specific technical commitments from non-technical contacts.
  • Meeting target: 12 accepted 20-minute meetings; at least 6 with direct buyers or insurer/programme teams; max 3 ecosystem-only validation meetings.
  • Daily cadence: 09:00 reply triage; 11:30 next-day reconfirmation; 16:30 CRM update; 17:30 top-5 next-day target decision.
  • Disqualify fast: no named workflow owner, no timing/procurement clue, innovation-only scout, incumbent owns workflow end-to-end, or no cross-system exception/audit pain.
  • Logistics note: official event is HLTH Europe 2026, 15–18 June 2026, RAI Convention Centre Amsterdam; RAI public address is Europaplein 24, 1078 GZ Amsterdam and central phone is +31 (0)20 549 12 12. Use venue phone only for logistics/verification, not prospecting.

15-minute collector delta — source-strength gate for Sales-People calls

Add four separate evidence scores to every 10-account pilot call note before booking a meeting:

  • policy_signal_strength: official / sector media / weak.
  • procurement_signal_strength: named tender or funding route / programme clue / none.
  • technology_stack_strength: named platform or integration surface / generic IT / unknown.
  • owner_evidence_strength: named role/team / routed contact only / unknown.

Booking guardrail: Sales-People may book a Truviq workflow-fit slot only when at least two of the four fields are above “weak/unknown” and the prospect can name a workflow owner or implementation route. Otherwise log as nurture/research, not meeting.

15-minute collector delta — call outcome taxonomy

Use one mutually exclusive sales_people_outcome value per contacted record so the hourly/deep job can score the campaign without rereading free-text notes:

  • booked_workflow_fit: meeting booked with named workflow/programme owner or credible implementation route.
  • routed_to_owner: no meeting yet, but prospect gave the correct team/person/route to validate next.
  • nurture_evidence_gap: relevant account, but missing procurement, owner, stack or timing evidence.
  • partner_only: useful ecosystem/implementation partner, not a direct buyer.
  • disqualified_noise: innovation-only, no workflow owner, no cross-system exception/audit pain, or incumbent owns workflow end-to-end.

Minimum note fields for any non-disqualified outcome: programme_hook, owner_or_route, current_platform_supplier, timing_or_procurement_clue, HLTH_or_June_availability, next_action. This keeps Sales-People useful as evidence collection rather than generic appointment setting.

15-minute collector delta — AZWA chain-approach funding gate

For AZWA chain-approach regional governance records, Sales-People should not treat “AZWA” as a budget signal by itself. Add one mandatory verification question before booking a Truviq slot:

  • “Is this chain approach already tied to a named model plan, insurer transformation-funding route, regional coalition owner or upcoming tender/procurement step — or is it still policy/preparation only?”

Booking guardrail: only book a workflow-fit meeting when the answer identifies at least one of: model-plan owner, insurer programme route, regional coalition lead, implementation deadline, public procurement clue, or measurable reporting obligation. Otherwise classify the outcome as nurture_evidence_gap and ask the deep job to monitor official AZWA/model-plan publications.

Disqualifier: the opportunity is policy-only if no coalition owner, funding path, implementation date, cross-system handoff, or reporting/audit obligation can be named.

15-minute collector delta — post-HLTH proof-of-value gate

Add one post_hlth_pov_gate field to Sales-People-qualified opportunities before Truviq accepts them into a technical follow-up:

  • specific_workflow_named: prospect can name the workflow/care path/programme, not only “digital transformation”.
  • systems_boundary_visible: at least two systems/organisations are involved, e.g. portal/EHR/insurer/platform/regional partner.
  • exception_or_audit_pain: prospect can describe exception handling, escalation, evidence, compliance or reporting friction.
  • pov_owner_confirmed: named role/team owns a 4–6 week proof-of-value; routed names are enough for validation, not for budget assumption.

Follow-up rule: if fewer than three of the four are true, classify as nurture_evidence_gap or partner_only, even if the HLTH conversation was positive. This prevents the campaign from turning event enthusiasm into low-conversion technical workshops.

15-minute collector delta — meeting handoff packet

Before Truviq accepts a Sales-People-booked HLTH/June meeting, require a compact meeting_handoff_packet in the CRM/account note:

  • why_now: event, deadline, model-plan/AZWA route, procurement clue or implementation milestone.
  • workflow_boundary: the specific cross-system or cross-organisation handoff to test.
  • known_unknowns: budget owner, incumbent supplier, platform boundary and procurement route still unproven.
  • safe_opening_question: one non-confidential discovery question for the first 5 minutes.

Booking guardrail: if Sales-People cannot fill why_now and workflow_boundary, the meeting should remain tentative/research follow-up rather than consume Truviq technical capacity. This is especially important for ecosystem and pavilion contacts where enthusiasm is not the same as buyer fit.

Demolish check

  • Sales-People may lack specialist health-sector credibility; use them for access/qualification, not technical solution selling.
  • Calling health executives with a generic pitch will fail; scripts must be evidence-specific and role-specific.
  • HLTH attendees may be international and innovation-heavy, while Truviq’s best opportunities may sit with Dutch programme owners not attending.
  • NL Health~Holland Pavilion participants may be startups/scaleups seeking visibility, not buyers. Use pavilion participation as a routing clue only when it exposes named provider/payer meetings, implementation projects or partner-led workflows.
  • AZWA model plans may standardise solutions in ways that reduce room for bespoke Camunda work; Truviq must position as implementation/governance layer, not a competing application.
  • A positive HLTH meeting is not a pipeline opportunity unless it exposes a workflow boundary, exception/audit pain and a named proof-of-value owner.
